Unit 2, Outcome 1 1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Because copyright acts on all online or offline publications we publish, it certainly affects websites.
The law in the UK is the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 and it protects original creations involving literary work, music, art, film, and sound .[1]
For example, posting pictures taken by another person, writing by another person, videos of another person, music by another person without permission are illegal and are subject to legal restrictions.
Therefore, we should always get permission from the original author. Also we can also link the original site with permission.

Unit 2, Outcome 1 1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

There are various problems when people with disabilities use websites.
But the most representative problem is when blind people use websites.
But the growing awareness of the use of websites by people with disabilities, and the continuing development of various technologies for them, these two are very encouraging.
Three methods can be used for the visually impaired people.
The first method is to zoom in on the screen. If they have residual vision left, the screen enlargement can help them.
The second method is to sound out contents on the monitor. There is also inputting and running through voice sound on their computer.
Thirdly, there is a way to use a very special braille terminal device with a refreshable braille display to check the contents. However, it costs a lot of money.
Of the three methods, using a computer with sound is the least expensive.
Also, it has nothing to do with the existence of residual vision or ability to decipher braille. Therefore, it is the most widely used method of using computer for blind people.
But I don't think this is necessary for hearing impaired people because they can see it.

Unit 2, Outcome 1 1.7 Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

1)index.html: This file usually contains the contents of our homepage. These are the texts and images that people can see when they first visit our site. This can be created after creating a file called index.html, using a text editor.
2)images: This includes all the images to be used on our site.(e.g JPG/JPEG, PNG, GIF)
3)Styles: This includes CSS code for styling our content (e.g., setting letters and background colors).
4)scripts: This includes all JavaScript codes used to add features that interact on our website.
